Introduction to Chess

Chess is a two-player strategy board game of ancient origin. The game is played on a square board with 64 squares, arranged in an 8x8 grid. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king, meaning the king is under attack and cannot escape capture.

Benefits of Playing Chess

  • Improves Cognitive Skills: Enhances spatial awareness, improves memory, and boosts cognitive flexibility.
  • Boosts Academic Performance: Studies have shown that playing chess can lead to improved academic performance, especially in mathematics.
  • Enhances Strategic Thinking: Helps in developing critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
  • Supports Personal Growth: Encourages patience, persistence, and self-discipline.
